## Geometric definition of the derivatives

A derivative is an idea of approximation

![d-1](/static/images/calculus/d-1.png)

approximation: `p <--> Q`

Geometric definition

![d-2](/static/images/calculus/d-2.png)

infinite approximation means: $$\Delta x \to 0$$

$$
\lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x}
= \lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{f(x_0 + \Delta x) - f(x_0)}{\Delta x}
= f'(x_0)
$$

### Example 1

$$
f(x) = \frac{1}{x}
$$

**Derivative**:

$$

\lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{\Delta f}{\Delta x}
= \lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{\frac{1}{x + \Delta x} - \frac{1}{x}}{\Delta x}
= \lim_{\Delta x \to 0}\frac{x - (x + \Delta x)}{x \Delta x (x + \Delta x)}
= \lim_{\Delta x \to 0} - \frac{1}{x(x + \Delta x)}
$$

Take the *limit* as $\Delta \to 0$,

$$
\lim_{\Delta x \to 0} - \frac{x}{x(x + \Delta x)}
= - \frac{1}{x^2}
$$

Hence,

$$
f'(x) = - \frac{1}{x^2}
$$
